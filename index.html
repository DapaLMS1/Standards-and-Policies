These are guides on how to follow the laws and give practical advice on how to make workplaces safe. Following a Code of Practice shows you are trying to do the right thing. They can be found on the SafeWork NSW website .

It is considered very important to follow the codes of practice.

Australian Standards are agreed ways of doing things safely, made by experts. They set an expected level of safety.

Unless an Australian Standard is included in the Regulation, it is not an offence to not follow it. But courts accept that a reasonable person would meet this standard.

Examples for dental practices:

  • AS 5369-2023 – Replaces AS/NZS 4815:2006. It covers how to clean and sterilise reusable instruments in healthcare, including dental clinics.
  • Occupational Protective Footwear – Also part of AS 5369-2023.

More details can be found on the Standards Australia website .

These give advice on how to work safely in a specific job. They show what a reasonable person in that field would do to stay safe.

It’s important to follow these codes to show you are doing the right thing.

You can access useful Codes of Practice from:

  • Dental Board of Australia
  • Australian Dental Association

There are other laws you need to know and follow:

  • Poisons and Therapeutic Goods Act
  • Health Records and Information Privacy Act
  • EPA NSW Radiation Safety Practice Policies

Each workplace should also have its own Practice Policies – these are the rules for safety at work. Everyone, including bosses (PCBUs), workers, and visitors must follow them.

These documents help show you are trying to keep people safe:

  • Approved Codes of Practice
  • Australian Standards
  • Industry Codes of Practice
  • Practice Policies
